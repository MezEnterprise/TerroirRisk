The track record, before the verdict

Thirty-three vintages of Pétrus, 1987–2023. For each year the model predicts whether the wine lands above or below the historical mean of 94.9. The extremes are never wrong — the model is a detector of compromise, not an oracle of greatness.

A wet 1992 (SM 0.339) scored 90. A wetter 2024 (SM 0.355) scores ~95. Selection recovered four points the rain used to take. Ignore the two epochs and R²_oos collapses from 0.496 to 0.322. For any vintage from 2000 onward, the post-2000 coefficient is the honest one.

04

At Pomerol, less green means more money

ERA5 sees one weather pixel for all eight châteaux — it is blind to terroir. So we went to Sentinel-2, 10 metres, August–October. The chart below ranks the eight estates by Wine Advocate score against satellite vigour (NDRE). The correlation is r = −0.69. Negative. The greener the vine, the cheaper the wine.

Blue clay strangles the best vines into noble suffering: small canopy, concentrated fruit. Sandy soils give easy water, lush vegetation, lesser wine. The satellite does not measure vine health — it measures how much the vine suffers. Swap the château dummy for NDRE and a vineyard never seen in training is still predicted to 1.74 pt out of sample.

05

Why you can trust a number you cannot bribe

Public data only

ERA5-Land via Copernicus, Sentinel-2 L2A. No proprietary feed, no API key, no relationship with anyone who sells wine.

Sealed before scored

Every prediction is SHA-256 hashed and timestamped on Bitcoin before a critic tastes the vintage. The record predates the verdict.

Out-of-sample or nothing

Leave-one-vintage-out is the only metric we report. The tested year never enters its own training. No leakage, no flattery.

Falsified in the open

Heat, frost signatures, annual NDVI — all tested, all failed, all documented. The model's edges are published, not hidden.
